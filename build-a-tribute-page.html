Paolo Sorrentino

Italy’s most celebrated contemporary filmmaker

Here's a timeline of Paolo Sorrentino's life and career:

  • 1970 - Born in Naples, Italy
  • 1986 - Was orphaned after losing both of his parents
  • 1991 - Began working in the world of cinema as assistant director to Enzo Decaro for the film The Thieves of the Future (Ladri di Futuro)
  • 1998 - His first film as a screenwriter was released: The Dust of Naples (Polvere di Napoli). This year he also began directing short movies - Love has no Bounds (L'amore non ha confini)
  • 2001 - Made his first feature film: One Man up (L’uomo in più), with which he won Best New Director at the Venice Film Festival
  • 2004 - Achieved international recognition for The Consequences of Love (Le Conseguenze dell'Amore) - a psychological thriller starring Toni Servillo that won many awards
  • 2006 - His next feature - The Family Friend (L'amico di famiglia) - was shown at the Cannes Film Festival and the London Film Festival
  • 2008 - His following film, Il Divo, inspired by the figure of the controversial Italian politician Giulio Andreotti, won the Prix du Jury at Cannes Film Festival. Once again Tony Servillo played the leading role
  • 2010 - Published the novel Everybody's Right about an aging singer who abandons Italy for South America and struggles with the loss of his stardom. The novel was a bestseller in Italy and was nominated for Italy’s most prestigious literary award, Premio Strega
  • 2011 - Made his English-language feature debut with This Must Be the Place, a drama film that stars Sean Penn and Frances McDormand
  • 2013 - His next film, The Great Beauty (La Grande Bellezza), became an international success and won many awards, including the Oscar and the Golden Globe
  • 2015 - Premiere of Youth at the Cannes Film Festival, the second English-language film by Sorrentino, featuring Harvey Keitel, Michael Caine, Jane Fonda and Paul Dano. Also this year Sorrentino was awarded an Honorary Doctorate of Humane Letters at the American University of Rome
  • 2016 - Premiere of The Young Pope TV series starring Jude Law at the Venice International Film Festival - the first time in the history of the festival that a TV series has been a part of the program
  • 2018 - Another drama film, Loro, starring Toni Servillo, was released
  • 2019 - Premiere of The New Pope, a continuation of The Young Pope, starring Jude Law and John Malkovich
  • 2021 - His next work, an autobiographical film The Hand of God (È stata la mano di Dio), won the Grand Jury Prize at the Venice International Film Festival and was nominated for Best International Feature Film at the Academy Awards
  • As announced, Sorrentino will next direct the biopic Sue starring and produced by Jennifer Lawrence

You can read more about this talented filmmaker on his Wikipedia entry.
